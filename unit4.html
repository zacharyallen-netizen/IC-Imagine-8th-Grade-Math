4.1 Angle Relationships

🔑 COMPLEMENTARY: Two angles that add to 90°
🔑 SUPPLEMENTARY: Two angles that add to 180°
🔑 VERTICAL ANGLES: Opposite angles when lines cross → ALWAYS EQUAL
🔑 LINEAR PAIR: Adjacent angles on a line → Add to 180°

📝 Review: Worked Examples & Error Analysis

📝 Parallel lines: ∠1 = 3x + 15, ∠2 = 5x − 7 (alternate interior)

Step 1: Alternate interior angles are EQUAL: 3x + 15 = 5x − 7
Step 2: Subtract 3x: 15 = 2x − 7
Step 3: Add 7: 22 = 2x
Step 4: Divide by 2: x = 11
Step 5: Find angle: 3(11) + 15 = 48° ✓

🔍 Spot the Error: Triangle angles: 55°, 70°, and x

✗ Student work: 55 + 70 + x = 180 → 125 + x = 180 → x = 65°
✓ Fix: 180 − 125 = 55, not 65! Arithmetic error. Correct: x = 55°
💡 Double-check your subtraction, especially under time pressure.

🔍 Spot the Error: Parallel lines: corresponding angle 65°, find co-interior x

✗ Student work: Co-interior angles are equal → x = 65°
✓ Fix: Co-interior (same-side interior) angles are SUPPLEMENTARY, not equal. x = 180° − 65° = 115°
💡 Corresponding and alternate angles are EQUAL. Co-interior angles ADD to 180°.
